# Halo Effect

A psychological bias where a positive first impression carries forward to colour all subsequent judgements about a person, product, or website.

## In Web Design

> [!warning] You have half a second
> Research shows a visitor forms their first impression of a website in approximately **0.5 seconds** — before reading a single word of copy.

In that moment the visitor makes a gut-level judgement about whether the site is:
- Credible
- Trustworthy
- Worth their time

If the first impression is positive, they assume products are high quality, the team is competent, and give the benefit of the doubt throughout the rest of the visit.

If it is negative — or even neutral — the business is already fighting uphill.

## Implication: The Hero Section

The hero section (the first visible area of a page) is the single most important piece of real estate on a website. Most hero sections waste this moment with:
- Vague, generic headlines
- Stock photos of people in suits shaking hands
- No clear value proposition
