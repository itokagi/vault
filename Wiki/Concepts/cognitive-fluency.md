# Cognitive Fluency

The brain's preference for things that are easy to process. When a website feels clear and intuitive, users feel calm and capable. When it is cluttered or confusing, they feel overwhelmed and leave.

## The Principle

> [!note]
> The brain is wired to prefer things that are easy to process. Clarity creates calm. Confusion creates exit.

- **High fluency** → user feels smart, stays, converts
- **Low fluency** → cognitive load, user feels overwhelmed, bounces

## Declare War on Cognitive Load

Every section, every sentence, every design choice must be ruthlessly edited to reduce friction. The path to the desired outcome must be effortless.

Common cognitive load sources:
- Cluttered layouts with too many competing elements
- Jargon-heavy copy that makes users work to understand value
- Unclear navigation or no obvious next step
- Slow load times that disrupt the experience
