# Human-Made Design

A movement in web design towards deliberately crafted, brand-specific aesthetics as a direct reaction to the flood of AI-generated sites that all look the same.

## Why It Emerged

AI website builders (Wix, Squarespace, Hostinger) can generate a "clean and professional" site from a text prompt. The output is forgettable. When every site uses the same layout, fonts, stock imagery, and structure, businesses stop being brands and become commodities — competing on price alone.

> [!quote]
> "If you look like everyone else, you're going to be priced like everyone else."

## What It Looks Like

- Organic, asymmetric layouts that break the standard grid
- Expressive, distinctive typography
- Real photography of actual people — not stock photos or AI-generated images
- Texture, personality, and visual details that require a human to understand the brand

## The Business Case

When everyone's baseline is a 5-minute AI site, an intentional human design is what separates a website that converts from one that just exists. Differentiation is no longer optional — it is the product.
