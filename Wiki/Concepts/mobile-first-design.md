# Mobile-First Design

A design approach that treats the mobile experience as the primary, default version of a website — not an afterthought adapted from a desktop design.

## The Data

> [!warning] Mobile is the website
> - **60%** of all web traffic comes from mobile devices
> - During 2025 Cyber Week: **80%** of digital traffic and **70%** of all orders were on mobile

Most designers still design desktop-first and adapt for mobile afterwards. That approach is now completely backwards.

## What Mobile-First Requires

Designing for mobile must be baked in from the start, not retrofitted:
- **Touch targets** — buttons and links sized for fingers, not cursors
- **Load times** — lean, fast-loading assets; no heavy desktop images
- **Content hierarchy** — what matters most must come first on a small screen
- **Performance** — every animation, image, and third-party script has a mobile performance cost

## The Technical Consequence

Design decisions that seem purely aesthetic (number of images, complexity of animations, third-party scripts) have direct performance consequences — and performance consequences have direct business consequences via Google Core Web Vitals rankings and conversion rates.
