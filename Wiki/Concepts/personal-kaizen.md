# Personal Kaizen

The application of the [[kaizen|Kaizen philosophy]] to individual life — a structured strategy for fostering gradual, meaningful changes in productivity, well-being, and emotional balance.

## Key Ideas

- Treats personal development the same way organisations treat process improvement
- Encourages identifying areas for improvement and taking small, continuous actions that compound over time
- Not about dramatic life overhauls — about a progress-oriented mindset where every step builds toward lasting transformation

## Adopting the Mindset

- Celebrate small wins as genuine progress
- Build resilience — significant changes require consistency over time
- Establish a clear personal mission aligned with core values
- Manage energy wisely across all four dimensions (see [[kaizen-energy|Kaizen Energy]])

## Goal Setting

> [!tip] The Kaizen approach to goals
> Break large goals into smaller, measurable steps — "walk 15 minutes a day" not "improve health". Review and realign periodically as circumstances change.

> [!quote] Key insight
> "Life should be lived in sprints — intense bursts of effort followed by recovery — rather than as a never-ending marathon."

- Balance effort and recovery, not constant output
- Align goals with personal values and life purpose
