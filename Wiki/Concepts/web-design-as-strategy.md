# Web Design as Strategy

The shift from web design as a deliverable (a finished website) to web design as a business system that generates measurable outcomes — leads, sales, credibility, and trust.

## The Old Model vs The New

| Old | New |
|-----|-----|
| Deliver a finished website | Build a system that generates results |
| Focus on aesthetics | Apply psychology to guide user behaviour |
| Hand off to developer | Own performance and conversion outcomes |
| Talk about fonts and colours | Talk about conversion rates and business goals |

## What AI Cannot Do

AI can build a website. It cannot:
- Understand a business model and translate it into a lead-generation system
- Apply psychology to guide specific user behaviour
- Make the strategic call on what belongs in the hero section and why
- Diagnose why a site gets traffic but does not convert — and fix it
- Recognise that a sceptical audience needs social proof higher on the page

> [!tip] What the best designers sell
> Clients do not buy websites. They buy **more leads, more sales, more credibility, more trust**. The website is just the vehicle. Designers who understand this command a completely different price point.

## Accessibility as Strategy

75% of organisations report that better accessibility directly contributes to improved revenue. Treating accessibility as a core principle — not an afterthought — serves more users and future-proofs against tightening regulation.
