# Peak-End Rule

A psychological principle stating that people remember an experience based on its most intense moment (the peak) and how it ended — not on the average of all moments.

## In Web Design

Small details create memorable peaks of delight:
- Subtle animation when hovering over a button
- A small piece of visual feedback when completing a form
- A smooth, satisfying page transition

> [!tip] Why microinteractions matter
> These tiny details make a site feel alive. More importantly, they signal that someone **cared** about the user experience — and that perception of care translates directly into trust.

Trust is the single most important thing a website can earn from a visitor.

## Implication

Designers should intentionally engineer peak moments — places in the experience where the user feels a small burst of delight or satisfaction. The end of a key flow (a checkout, a form submission, a signup) deserves particular attention.
